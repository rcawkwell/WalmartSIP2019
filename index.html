Girls Who Code - Walmart - 2019

About

Girls Who Code is a non-profit organization that aims to increase the number of women in computer science. During the summer, Girls Who Code hosts 81 Summer Immersion Programs (SIPs). The SIP is a 7 week long camp that teaches girls how to code. The students learn Scratch, Python, Data Science, Web Development and Robotics. The camp is concluded with a two week long group project where the students create projects they are passionate about using their newly learned skills. Above all us, SIPs provide a safe, empowering and inclusive space for girls to be themselves.

Walmart has sponsored several SIPs throughout the country including: San Bruno, CA; Hoboken, NJ; Austin, TX; Bentonville, AR. This website is dedicated to the 2019 SIP located in Bentonville Arkansas.

Our Journey

Week One

Intro & Scratch

During the first week, we learned the fundamentals of computer science through the block programming language Scratch. The girls enjoyed how visual the programming language is. They built projects to introduce themselves to the other students. They created a maze, a choose your own adventure and a dance party!

Week Two

Python

During the second week, we moved from a block based language to text based language. We faced a lot of difficulties in learning the syntax of python. However through these challenges the girls bonded and played a lot of Mafia. They even wrote a python program to assist in the playing of Mafia!

U of A Trip

McMillion Innovation Studio

During the second week, we traveled to the McMillion Innovation Studio and Brewer Family Entrepreneurship Hub. There we received a tour of the University of Arkansas and participated in a workshop on entrepreneurship. The girls worked alongside the Fayetteville SIP to create prototypes to answer the question, how do we get more girls to code?

Week Three

Data Science

During the third week, we learned about the field of computer science and did projects in python. While this week faced many challenges, the girls enjoyed exploring their own data set on CORGIS. They were able to create data visulazations.

LPGA Day

Talk with Reshma

During the third week, we spent one afternoon at the LPGA. There we explored the STEAM Tent which included video games and AI robots. We then heard Reshma Saujani, CEO and founder of Girls Who Code, speak. She shared her story of founding GWC and answered questions from our students. The day ended with several short meetings with women in leadership positions who acted as mentors to the girls.

RevUnit Trip

Local Tech Company

During the third week, we traveled to RevUnit, a Bentonville based app consulting company. They told us about the company, did a Q&A and led a workshop on entrepreneurship. Students worked in groups to create solutions around how to improve the SIP.

Week Four

Web Development

Week Four was a short week as we celebrated the Fourth and Fifth of July with no class. During this week, girls learned about HTML, CSS and Javascript. Many of the students started to appreciate the complexity of creating well designed websites.

Week Five

Robotics

During week five, our students learned about robotics. We discussed the implications of AI and debated if robots were good or bad. The students learned about breadboards and completed projects such as making their robots dance and be able to navigate through a maze.

Mentor Day

Talk with Jae Evans

During week five, we spent a day doing more professional development. We had someone from Walmart's HR come and discuss how to create a good Resume. The girls got to talk to a range of female mentors and ask questions. We then heard from Jae Evans, an SVP at Walmart. The day ended with a celebration with Kona Ice.

Week Six + Seven

Final Projects

During the last two weeks, the girls created their own groups and worked on a personal project. They were encouraged to create projects that had an impact and solved a problem. They were able to work in any language and as a result we had projects in Scratch, Python and HTML/CSS/JS.

Graduation

Walton Arts Center

On July 25th, friends, family and Walmart/P&G employees were invited to celebrate the completion of the SIP. The event was from 6-9pm at the Walton Arts Center in Fayetteville, AR. The girls created posters and a 1 minute pitch. We heard from two keynote speakers: Kristin Williams & Megan Timberlake. We also heard from two self elected class speakers. Isabelle Paulsen gave Walmart's Class address
